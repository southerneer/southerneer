I've played drums since middle school but I'd never taken it seriously until about a year ago. I did marching band through 10th grade and my parents bought me a beginner-level drumkit that I pounded on every once in a while. When I went off to college at UNC I couldn't easily bring the set with me into my dorm room...at least not at first. My kit actually lived in a buddy's dorm room during my senior year and I would go by to jam occasionally but that was still pretty far from "serious".

After college my drum set would always make the move with me from rental houses in Chapel Hill (Bolinwood, Glendale) to Raleigh (Kaplan, White Oak) annoying many a roommate along the way. When I finally migrated back to Charlotte I started jamming with my friend Jason Rome. He introduced me to other musicians in the area and we would get together every couple of weeks to jam. I still wasn't practicing on a regular basis at this point, but the interaction with other musicians definitely heightened my interest in improving and growing as a drummer.

Last year (2012), Jason joined Trial by Fire, a local Journey tribute band, as their new keyboard player and backup vocalist. This was a legit band that played at places like Amos' Southend and The Fillmore -- large local venues. I was really happy for him, dropped him a pound, and didn't think too much more of it. That was the spring of 2012 when I didn't have a whole lot going on...I was still recovering from my second shoulder surgery so all the athletic activities that used to take up a large part of my extracurricular time were now out of the question. Back in 2011 I bought an electronic drumset that would allow me to practice in my downtown Charlotte condo without annoying the piss out of my neighbors. I started to practicing regularly and more methodically. Having an electronic kit had the added benefit of being much more portable and allowed me to Â jam with other musicians much more easily. Later in 2012, Jason approached me about trying out for Trial by Fire as they were seeking a new drummer. I thought "what the hell", tried out and made it! I've never been a huge Journey fan per se, but I really started to appreciate their music a lot more as I started learning up on the songs. Also, I gained a new appreciation for musicians who perform live. There are many aspects of putting on a show (sound, lighting, equipment, transportation, stage presence) that I'd never properly considered before I was actually up there.

Flash forward to the present. I'm still a member of Trial by Fire, but I'm transitioning out of the band to move on to other ventures (more on that later). In the meantime, I'm playing with a more traditional cover band, Five on Sundays, that is just getting off the ground. This is another Jason Rome joint (do you sense a theme here?) that also features a number of other guys (Zak Foltz and Mark Miller) with whom I had previously jammed since moving to Charlotte. We've played two shows and we're looking forward to a few more in the next couple months.

The moral of this ramble? I don't know. I've been messing around with my various websites lately and felt like putting up some fresh content. So sue me.
