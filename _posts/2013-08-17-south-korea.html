My friends,
If you didn't already know, I'm moving to South Korea! I'll be shipping out on September 21st with my lady and we'll be gone for at least a year. Whitney will be teaching Engrish to little Korean children in Incheon and I'll be working with my old buddy Clay.

This move promises to be many things...

A Bummer

...because I'll no longer get to jam out with Trial by Fire or 5 on Sundays. This past year has been an amazing time when I've gotten to play drums in both of these bands. In TBF, I had the challenge of meshing with a well-established group and learning all the ins and outs of playing a live show. After just one month of small weekend gigs I was playing on the big stage at The Fillmore in front of a thousand people! In 5OS, I participated in the start of a brand new band that is rapidly gaining recognition in the Charlotte live music scene. We started practicing in earnest in January of this year and we've already played 5 shows in front of hundreds of people! I've made lifelong friends in my bandmates from each group and I'll really miss the experience of playing with them.

I'll also be leaving my friends and family in the Charlotte area. I won't be able to grab lunch with the uptown working folks anymore. No more Wednesday basketball at Charlotte Prep. No more haircut clubs. No more Bobcats/Hornets games. No more grilling on the patio. Sad face.

A Challenge

...because my world will be changing entirely. I'm taking on a new and exciting role with Clay's startup company, Redwood. I'll have to learn the language. I'll have to figure out how to get around in one of the largest cities in the world. I'll have to cope with a culture that thinks "barbecue" means this instead of this and has never even heard of this. Apprehensive face.

Awesome

...because I get to live out a lifelong dream! I never studied abroad in college and while I've traveled all over the US and even to different countries I've never lived in a foreign country. There's always been a good excuse to not do it, but as I approach 30 years on this earth it's become clear that I need to shit or get off the pot. And who better to shit with than my lovely girlfriend Whitney?! (phrasing could use a little work there, but you get the gist).

Are you gone forever?

Nope, just a year initially. In fact, Whitney and I will both be forced to leave Korea after a year because our work visas will expire. And while we'll physically be on the other side of the world we'll still be easily reachable. There's always email, Facebook, and Twitter. And I have a permanent phone number thanks to Google Voice. And Whitney and I will (hopefully) be composing some video messages of our new experiences in Korea. It's only a year and it'll fly by before you know it, but in the meantime I'll miss my friends and family back home dearly.

One really good way of alleviating the pain of parting: come visit us! We won't have a ton of room (I'll be rooming with Clay and Whitney will be cozied up in a studio apartment) but if you can stomach the airfare and sleeping on a couch then we will show you an amazing time in South Korea where the food is tasty and cheap, the beer is plentiful, the soju even more plentiful, the people adore Americans, and you won't get shot!
